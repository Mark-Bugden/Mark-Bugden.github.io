About Me

I am a researcher with a background in mathematics and theoretical physics. My PhD and subsequent postdoctoral research was based on string theory and higher-dimensional black holes. I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Animal Behavior where I am using Machine Learning to model the collective movement of animals.


I am proficient in Python and the usual datascience packages, including Tensorflow, Seaborn, Pandas, Matplotlib, and Numpy. I am also familiar with common mathematical and typesetting software such as Mathematica, Maple, Matlab, and LaTeX.

You can find more information about the academic side of my career at my personal webpage.
